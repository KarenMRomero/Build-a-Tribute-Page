PhD. Ángela Camacho

Brief Biography

For science, Doctor in Physics Ángela Stella Camacho Beltrán has dedicated her whole life to the infinitely small and, out of conscience, now has the great challenge of promoting science-based female entrepreneurship that contributes to the development of the country.

The PhD was born in 1947 in Bogotá, where she studied with the nuns of the Colegio La Presentación, but it was Professor Quiroga who made her fall in love with physics during a class in which he clearly explained the concepts of velocity and acceleration.

Since she liked teaching since she was a child, she entered the National University of Colombia with the idea of pursuing a degree, but graduated as a physicist in 1970. Her career in this dicipline of science was just beginning. Of her 40 classmates, only ten managed to finish their studies and three, including her, were women

"Mija, don't study so much that you'll go crazy," her mother told her. However, the 'studying' and the 'madness', which was not easy for her because her classmates had a better grounding in mathematics, allowed her to win a scholarship to study for a master's degree in nuclear physics at the Darmstadt University of Technology, which seemed to her to be the most advanced at the time. When he finished his master's degree in 1973, he realized that in Colombia there was little he could do with nuclear physics and thought that he would do much more with condensed matter and solid state physics, since developing it required fewer resources.

Therefore, in 1974, when she was offered a scholarship for her doctorate, she chose to do it in condensed matter, in liquids. In 1977 she became the first Colombian woman to obtain a doctoral degree in physics, awarded by the Johannes Gutenberg University of Mainz (Germany). When she returned to Colombia, she was offered a job at the Universidad Central de Caracas or at the Universidad Industrial de Santander (UIS), which had a master's degree but no undergraduate degree in physics. He opted to stay in Colombia. Other early PhDs, also trained and graduated in Germany, returned to the Universidad Nacional or went to France, Israel and the United States.

" I am currently very focused on something that I feel is like my obligation. I want to give back to society everything it has given me because I have been really benefited a lot. One way is to work for women and another is a little bit in science outreach. "

Awards and Recognitions

  • Scientific Production Award, COLCIENCIAS, 1990.
  • Member of the COLCIENCIAS researcher incentive program, 1994.
  • Member of the program of incentives to researchers of COLCIENCIAS, 1996.
  • Member of the winning group of the Special Call of COLCIENCIAS for groups of excellence, 1996.
  • Associate Professor, Universidad del Valle, Cali-Colombia, 1997-1999.
  • Member of the Condensed Matter Physics Group, Group ranked by COLCIENCIAS in category A, 1999.
  • Member of the Condensed Matter Physics Group, Group ranked by COLCIENCIAS in category A, 2001.
  • Leader for Colombia for the Women in Physics movement, Paris, March 2002.
  • Leader for Colombia of the Microelectronics subprogram of CYTED, 1999-2003.
  • National Award for Excellence in Research, Colombian Association for the Advancement of Science (ACAC), 2003.
  • Guest Editor, Microelectronics Journal, Vol.9 No.11, November 2008.
  • Leader for Colombia of the CYTED NANODYF network, 2011-2014.
